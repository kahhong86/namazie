A patented methodology for learning music

PATENTS showcase

A patent can be obtained for novel inventions. Patents can cover mechanical, chemical or electronic inventions. Even a new method of performing a series of actions, or conducting business, is potentially patentable. One such patent was obtained by an accomplished musician who secured a patent for a “System and Method for Facilitating Learning of a Musical Instrument”. This novel methodology teaches us to play a musical instrument even without a musical background and without the instruction of a music teacher. By merely following the patented methodology for music learning re-defined (patent pending in the USA, UK and Australia), a student can potentially play and record a musical piece. The methodology is available in an online platform that facilitates the learning of a musical instrument such as the piano, guitar etc. The Inventor, Samana Holdings Pte Ltd, is a Singapore company.
